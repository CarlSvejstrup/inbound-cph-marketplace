# Inbound CPH house voice

This is the voice for **Inbound's own** content: proposals, case studies, agency Slack, internal docs, recruiting copy, the website. **Not** for client work, each client has their own `01-brand/voice.md` that overrides this one.

## Principles

1. **Evidence over enthusiasm.** Numbers, specifics, dated examples. "92% of early buyers re-rated 5 stars after one trip" beats "customers love it."
2. **Nordic restraint by default.** Understated, calm, factual. The fact does the work. Avoid superlatives, hype words, and exclamation marks.
3. **Show the craft.** We are proud of the work. Talk about the *how* (the experiment, the iteration, the data) more than the *what*.
4. **Specialist, not generalist.** We are four specialised teams (SEM, SEO, Social, Tracking), not "full-service digital." Talk like a specialist.
5. **B2B Nordic register.** Professional but human. Not corporate. Not casual. Definitely not American agency-speak.

## Banned constructions

- "Game-changing", "revolutionary", "best-in-class", "world-class"
- "Unlock", "supercharge", "elevate", "10x"
- "We are passionate about", show it, do not claim it
- "Trusted by leading brands", name them or skip it
- Opening with "In today's fast-paced digital landscape..." or any variant

## Sentence shapes

- Short sentences. Vary rhythm but bias short.
- Lead with the fact, not the framing.
- One idea per sentence.
- Numbers in figures (92%, not "ninety-two percent").
- Dates as "2026-04" or "April 2026", never "Q2 of this year".

## When in doubt

If a draft would feel out of place in a Nordic boardroom, rewrite it. If it sounds like a US SaaS landing page, rewrite it. If it sounds like the team writing it would be embarrassed to say it out loud at lunch, rewrite it.
